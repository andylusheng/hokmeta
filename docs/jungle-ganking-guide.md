# Jungle Ganking Guide: When, Where, and How to Secure Kills

*by Marcus Tran · Updated June 2026 · 9 min read*

---

Ganking is the jungler's most visible contribution. Your laners don't see you clearing camps. They don't see you tracking the enemy jungler. They only see you when you show up in their lane — and if you show up and don't get a kill, they'll remember that.

Here's how to gank effectively: when to do it, which lanes to prioritize, and how to execute ganks that actually result in kills.

---

## The Golden Rules of Ganking

### 1. Only Gank When You Have a Reasonable Chance of Success

If the enemy is full HP, standing under their tower, with Flash up, and you have no CC — don't gank. You're wasting time and revealing your position.

### 2. Gank the Lane That's Most Vulnerable

Not the lane that's pinging the most. Not the lane where your duo partner is playing. The lane where the enemy is pushed past river with no escape.

### 3. Don't Force Ganks

If you walk into a lane and the enemy immediately backs off, leave. They saw you coming (probably a ward). Don't stand there waiting — you're not going to catch them, and the enemy jungler is now free to gank the opposite side of the map.

---

## Which Lane to Gank

### Gank Farm Lane (Bottom) When:
- The enemy ADC is pushed up
- Your support has CC (Donghuang, Zhang Fei)
- Dragon is spawning soon (gank → kill → Dragon)
- The enemy ADC has no Flash

### Gank Mid Lane When:
- The enemy mage is pushed past river
- Your mid laner has CC (Angela, Daji)
- You're pathing from one side of the jungle to the other (Mid is on the way)

### Gank Clash Lane (Top) When:
- The enemy Clash Laner is overextended
- Your Clash Laner has CC (Mulan, Guan Yu)
- Lord is spawning soon (gank → kill → Lord)

## How to Execute a Gank

### The Standard Gank

1. **Approach from behind.** Walk through river or enemy jungle to come from behind the enemy. Don't walk straight down the lane — they'll see you and run.

2. **Ping "on my way" before you arrive.** Give your laner a second to position.

3. **Let your laner engage first (if possible).** If your laner has CC, let them land it before you show yourself. The enemy will commit to fighting your laner, and then you appear behind them.

4. **Use your CC to lock them down.** If you have a stun, knockup, or slow, use it to prevent their escape.

5. **Don't dive unless you're sure.** Tower shots hurt. Only dive if the enemy is low enough to die in one or two hits.

### The Lane Gank (Walking Through Lane)

Instead of coming from river, walk through the lane bushes while the enemy can't see you. This only works if your laner has pushed the wave to the enemy tower — you can sneak into the lane bushes without being seen.

When the wave pushes back toward your tower, the enemy will walk forward to farm. That's when you strike.

### The Counter-Gank

If you see the enemy jungler heading toward one of your lanes, rotate there immediately. Don't engage first — wait for the enemy jungler to commit to the gank, then show up and turn it into a 2v2 or 3v3 where you have the element of surprise.

Counter-ganks are the most impactful plays a jungler can make. A successful counter-gank results in kills, tower pressure, and a demoralized enemy jungler.

---

## Ganking with Specific Heroes

### Assassins (Fatih, Lam, Li Bai, Prince of Lanling)
You're the primary damage dealer. Wait for your laner to engage or CC the enemy, then jump in and burst them down. Don't engage first unless you can 100-0 them.

### Fighters (Musashi, Sun Ce, Zilong, Kaizer)
You can engage first. Use your durability to absorb the enemy's initial cooldowns, then let your laner follow up.

### Tanks (Dun jungle, Arthur jungle)
Your ganks are about CC, not damage. Lock the enemy down and let your laner do the damage.

---

## What to Do After a Successful Gank

1. **Push the wave.** Help your laner push the minion wave into the enemy tower. This denies the enemy gold and experience while your laner recalls.

2. **Take tower plates if possible.** Early tower damage adds up.

3. **Look for objectives.** If Dragon or Lord is up, rotate there with your team.

4. **Don't overstay.** Take what you can and leave. The enemy jungler is probably pathing toward you.

---

## Common Ganking Mistakes

1. **Showing too early.** If the enemy sees you before you're in range, they'll run. Approach from fog of war.

2. **Ganking a lane that's losing hard.** If your Clash Laner is 0/3 and two levels behind, ganking their lane might result in a double kill — for the enemy.

3. **Not checking enemy summoner spells.** If the enemy has Flash up, they can escape your gank. Track summoner cooldowns.

4. **Forcing ganks when your ult is down.** Most junglers need their ultimate to secure kills. Wait for it.

5. **Ignoring objective timers.** A gank is worth less than a Dragon. If Dragon is spawning in 15 seconds, path toward Dragon, not the opposite side of the map.

---

## Quick Takeaways

- Gank lanes where the enemy is pushed up and your laner can follow
- Approach from behind, ping before you arrive, let your laner engage first
- Counter-ganks are the highest-impact plays
- After a successful gank: push wave, take plates, rotate to objectives
- Don't gank losing lanes or force ganks without your ultimate
