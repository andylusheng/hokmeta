# Han Xin Jungle Guide: The Spear That Carries Every Game (If You Let It)

*by Marcus Tran · Updated June 2026 · 10 min read*

---

Han Xin is that hero everyone has an opinion on. Half the playerbase thinks he's a troll pick. The other half has been absolutely destroyed by a Han Xin one-trick who spent the entire game in their jungle, stole every buff, and ended 14/1.

The truth is somewhere in between — but closer to the second one. Han Xin is a high-ceiling jungler with one of the fastest clear speeds in the game and mobility that makes Li Bai look like he's walking. If you're willing to put in the games, he can solo carry harder than almost anyone in Honor of Kings.

I've played over 200 games on Han Xin across three accounts. Here's what actually works.

---

## Why Play Han Xin?

Three reasons:

1. **Clear speed.** Han Xin farms faster than almost every jungler in the game. More farm = more items = more impact. It's that simple.
2. **Mobility.** Between Skill 1 (two charges of a dash that also knocks people up) and Skill 2 (a backward dash), you're almost never caught. You can invade, steal camps, and leave before the enemy team even reacts.
3. **Split push threat.** Han Xin takes towers faster than most marksmen. If your team is losing teamfights, you can just win through map pressure instead.

The downside? He's squishy, his teamfight is situational, and if you fall behind early, you're going to have a very bad time.

---

## Skills Breakdown

### Passive: Killing Spear

Every fourth basic attack knocks the target up. Skills and enhanced basic attacks give you bonus attack speed.

This passive is why Han Xin can outduel people he has no business beating. The knockup on your fourth auto is free CC — and with the attack speed buff from your skills, you're hitting that fourth auto very fast. A lot of Han Xin players don't even realize they're getting knockups mid-fight. Pay attention to your auto count. It matters.

### Skill 1: Ruthless Assault

Two charges. Each charge dashes to a target location, deals physical damage, and the first charge knocks up enemies. The second charge does damage but no knockup.

This is your engage, your escape, your gap closer, your wall hop. The knockup on the first charge is a real CC tool — you can cancel channeled abilities, interrupt dashes, and set up your ult with it.

One tip that separates good Han Xin players from average ones: **don't burn both charges at once unless you're 100% sure you're getting the kill.** Save one charge to get out.

### Skill 2: Fight or Die!

Backward dash. Your next basic attack becomes enhanced, dealing bonus damage in a sweep. If this sweep is your fourth basic attack, it inherits the knockup effect.

This skill is weird at first because it dashes you backward — away from where you're facing. You get used to it. What matters is the enhanced auto, which hits in an arc and can apply your passive knockup if timed correctly.

Use it to: reposition in fights, dodge skillshots, jump over walls, and most importantly, reset your auto-attack timer to proc your passive faster.

### Skill 3 (Ultimate): The Unrivaled Spear

Han Xin spins his spear four times in place. During the channel, he gains damage reduction and is immune to crowd control. The final hit knocks enemies up.

This is your teamfight button. The CC immunity is the real value — you can walk into a fight, pop ult, eat whatever CC they throw at you, and still get your damage off. The damage reduction (30% at max rank) makes you surprisingly tanky for an assassin.

Don't use this on jungle camps unless you're desperate. Save it for fights.

---

## The Combo

Basic engage:

**Skill 1 (first charge, knockup) → auto → Skill 2 (backward dash) → enhanced auto → Skill 1 (second charge) → auto → Ultimate**

The idea is to weave autos between every skill to maximize your passive procs. If you do it right, you'll get at least two knockups (from passive and from Skill 1) before they can fight back.

For ganking a lane with CC:

**Walk in → auto → Skill 1 → auto → Skill 2 → enhanced auto → Ultimate → Skill 1 to chase or escape**

Let your laner's CC land first if possible. Han Xin's ganks are much scarier when the enemy is already slowed or rooted.

## Recommended Build

### Standard Carry Build

1. Hunter Boots — early gank speed
2. Spiked Shell — attack speed and passive damage, great for farming
3. Blade of Eternity — physical pen and damage
4. Endless Blade — this is where your crits start hurting
5. Shadow Ripper — armor pen + cooldown reduction
6. Bloodweeper or Cloak of Destiny — sustain vs. more damage

### Split Push Build (when your team can't win fights)

1. Boots of Deftness — more map movement speed
2. Spiked Shell
3. Blade of Eternity
4. Endless Blade
5. Shadow Ripper
6. Longbow of Ages — extra tower damage and late-game scaling

---

## Arcana Setup

- Hunting (x10) — attack speed and movement speed
- Eagle Eye (x10) — armor pen
- Cataclysm (x10) — crit chance and crit damage

Some players swap Cataclysm for more flat attack damage if they want a stronger early game. I prefer crit because Han Xin's attack speed is already high — more crits mean more passive procs, which means more knockups.

## Jungle Clear Order

Han Xin's clear speed is S-tier. You can full clear and be at level 4 before almost anyone.

**Red Buff → Raptors → Wolves → Blue Buff → Toad → level 4 → gank**

Why Red Buff start? Because Han Xin's level 2 with Red Buff is actually scary. If the enemy jungler started Blue and you spot them on the map, you can invade their Blue side after your Red and steal at least one camp — or kill them if they're low.

One thing: **Han Xin doesn't need Blue Buff as much as other junglers.** His cooldowns are already short and his clear is fast enough without it. Don't die contesting Blue if you're behind.

## When to Pick Han Xin (And When Not To)

### Pick Han Xin when:
- Enemy team has a slow jungler you can invade (think Dian Wei, Butterfly early)
- Your team has strong lanes that don't need babysitting — you want to farm, not gank every 30 seconds
- The enemy comp lacks point-and-click CC (if they have Daji or Angela mid, be careful)
- You need split push pressure more than teamfight presence

### Don't pick Han Xin when:
- Enemy has Fatih, Musashi, or Sun Ce — they match your clear speed and outduel you
- Your team picked four squishies and expects you to frontline
- Enemy has layered CC (Da Qiao, Guan Yu, Donghuang) that shuts down your mobility
- You're first-timing him in ranked. Please don't.

---

## The Split Push Mindset

This is where Han Xin truly shines. His wave clear is fast, his tower damage is high, and his mobility means you can push a side lane, force someone to respond, then rotate to the opposite lane before they can catch you.

The playbook:

1. Push side lane until you see someone on the minimap rotating to stop you
2. Leave immediately — don't greed for the tower if they're close
3. Rotate to the opposite lane or take enemy jungle camps while they waste time walking
4. Repeat until the enemy team is so spread out your team wins the next fight for free

This works at every rank. Even in Mythic, people fall for it.

---

## Quick Takeaways

- Farm first, fight second. Han Xin with an item lead is a different hero.
- Save one Skill 1 charge for escape unless the kill is guaranteed.
- Use your ultimate to tank CC in teamfights — the immunity is more valuable than the damage.
- Split push when teamfights aren't working. You're faster than everyone.
- Don't pick him into Fatih or Sun Ce. Seriously.

Han Xin is a monster if you commit to him. The first 30 games will feel terrible. Game 31 is when it clicks.
