# Best Heroes for Beginners in Honor of Kings: Easy to Learn, Strong to Play

*by Marcus Tran · Updated June 2026 · 8 min read*

---

Picking your first hero in Honor of Kings is like picking your first Pokemon — you want something that looks cool, but also something that won't get you destroyed in your first game.

Here are the best heroes for beginners in each role. These heroes are simple to learn, forgiving of mistakes, and strong enough to win games while you're still figuring out what the buttons do.

---

## Farm Lane (ADC): Hou Yi

**Why he's good for beginners:**
- Simple kit: auto-attack, auto-attack, auto-attack
- Passive slows enemies, making it easy to kite
- Ultimate is a global stun that helps your team even if you're on the other side of the map
- Teaches good positioning habits (no dash = you learn to position or you die)

**What you'll learn:** Last-hitting, positioning, map awareness (global ult)

**Avoid if:** You want mobility. Hou Yi has zero dashes.

---

## Mid Lane: Angela

**Why she's good for beginners:**
- Straightforward combo: stun, fireball, laser beam
- High burst damage — if you land your stun, you kill them
- Ultimate gives a shield, so you're harder to kill while casting
- Teaches skill-shot aiming and bush camping

**What you'll learn:** Skill-shot accuracy, combo execution, roaming

**Avoid if:** You want a high-skill-ceiling mage. Angela is simple and effective but one-dimensional.

---

## Clash Lane: Arthur

**Why he's good for beginners:**
- Free hero — everyone has Arthur
- Tanky enough to survive mistakes
- Simple abilities: spin, dash, execute
- Passive heals you over time, so you can stay in lane longer
- Works in multiple roles (Clash, Jungle, Support)

**What you'll learn:** Trading in lane, when to engage, basic macro

**Avoid if:** You want flashy plays. Arthur is the definition of reliable but boring.

---

## Support: Cai Yan

**Why she's good for beginners:**
- Healing support — your job is to keep your team alive
- Simple abilities: heal, stun, team-wide heal
- Doesn't need to engage or make plays
- Hard to mess up — even if you position badly, you're still healing
- Teaches map awareness and team fight positioning

**What you'll learn:** Map awareness, peeling for carries, vision control

**Avoid if:** You want to be the playmaker. Cai Yan enables others but doesn't make plays herself.

---

## Jungle: Butterfly

**Why she's good for beginners:**
- Simplest jungler in the game
- Passive resets her abilities on kill or assist — great for team fights
- Decent clear speed, forgiving if you mess up your pathing
- Doesn't require complex combos — just auto-attack and use abilities

**What you'll learn:** Jungle pathing, gank timing, objective control

**Avoid if:** You want to be a high-impact carry jungler. Butterfly is consistent but not flashy.

---

## Heroes to Avoid as a Beginner

Some heroes are amazing in the right hands but will make you hate the game if you start with them:

- **Li Bai** — Requires precise combo execution and passive management
- **Han Xin** — Needs fast farming and map pressure to be effective
- **Arli** — Highest skill ceiling marksman; one wrong reposition and you're dead
- **Fatih** — Best jungler but requires aggressive invading and perfect timing
- **Da Qiao** — Her ultimate can teleport your entire team into a losing fight

---

## Quick Takeaways

- Start with Hou Yi, Angela, Arthur, Cai Yan, or Butterfly
- Learn one role before branching out
- Avoid high-skill-ceiling heroes until you understand the game
- The best beginner hero is the one you enjoy playing
