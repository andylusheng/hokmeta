# Honor of Kings Team Fight Guide: 7 Mistakes That Lose Team Fights

*by Marcus Tran · Updated June 2026 · 11 min read*

---

Team fights in Honor of Kings are where games are won and lost. You can win every lane, take every tower, and be up 5k gold — then lose one bad team fight at the 18-minute mark and watch the enemy team march down mid and end the game.

I've thrown more games than I'd like to admit by taking bad fights. Here are the seven most common team fight mistakes and how to stop making them.

---

## Mistake 1: Fighting Without Your Ultimate

This sounds obvious. It's not. I still see Diamond players engaging when their ultimate is on a 15-second cooldown.

Your ultimate is usually your strongest ability. For some heroes — Lu Bu, Da Qiao, Zhang Fei, Sun Ce — it defines their entire team fight presence. If you don't have it, don't fight. Ping your team, back off, and wait.

**Fix:** Check your team's ultimate cooldowns before every fight. The little green dots next to your teammates' portraits on the top of the screen tell you who has their ult ready. If two or more teammates don't have ults, you're fighting at a massive disadvantage.

---

## Mistake 2: Fighting When You're Behind on Gold

This is the most common throw in Honor of Kings. Your team is down 3k gold, the enemy just took Dragon, and someone on your team decides now is the perfect time to 5v5 in the river.

Spoiler: it's not. When you're behind, you need picks — isolated enemies you can catch and kill to even the odds. You don't need fair 5v5s because those aren't fair when you're behind.

**Fix:** When you're behind, play defensively. Ward your jungle. Look for enemy mistakes. Catch someone rotating alone. Turn a 5v5 into a 5v4, then fight.

---

## Mistake 3: The Support Engages While the ADC Is Farming

I see this every single day. The support — usually a Lian Po or Dun — sees a "perfect engage" and dives the enemy backline. Meanwhile, their ADC is two screens away farming a minion wave.

The support dies. The team flames them. The support types "where is my team??" in chat. Nobody wins.

**Fix:** Before you engage, look at your minimap. Where are your damage dealers? If they're not in range to follow up within two seconds, don't go in. Ping "on my way" before engaging so your team knows you're about to do something stupid — I mean, brave.

---

## Mistake 4: Tunneling on the Wrong Target

Every team has that one player who will chase the enemy support across the entire map while their own backline gets deleted by the enemy jungler. Don't be that player.

As a general rule: your frontline should be hitting their frontline, and your damage dealers should be hitting whoever is closest. The exception is assassins and divers — their job is to bypass the frontline and kill the enemy carries.

**Fix:** Know your role. If you're the tank, your job is to absorb damage and apply CC — not to chase kills. If you're the ADC, hit whoever is in range. If you're the assassin, wait for the enemy backline to be vulnerable, then strike.

---

## Mistake 5: Blowing All Your Cooldowns on the Tank

This is the classic low-elo team fight. Enemy Zhang Fei walks forward. Your entire team dumps every ability on him. He ults, transforms, and walks away with 60% HP. Meanwhile, the enemy Fatih has been quietly walking around the back and just deleted your ADC and mage.

Tanks want you to hit them. That's their job. Don't give them what they want.

**Fix:** Save your burst for the enemy carries. If you're a mage with a stun (Angela, Daji), hold it for the enemy assassin or marksman. If you're a fighter, look for angles to reach the backline instead of slapping the tank.

---

## Mistake 6: Not Respecting the Enemy's Power Spikes

Some heroes are just stronger at certain points in the game. Lam at level 4 with his ultimate is terrifying. Lu Bu at three items is a different hero. Kongming at full build can 1v5 if he plays it right.

If you're fighting into a power spike, you're going to lose — even if you're ahead on gold.

**Fix:** Learn the power spikes of common meta heroes. When Lam hits level 4, play safe. When the enemy Lu Bu has three items, don't clump up. When Kongming is full build, end the game before he can scale.

---

## Mistake 7: Not Ending When You Win the Fight

You just aced the enemy team. It's 45 seconds until they respawn. What do you do?

If you said "recall and buy items," we need to talk. If you said "take Baron and push towers," you're correct.

Too many teams win a fight, get excited, and forget to actually win the game. Every team fight you win should translate into an objective — a tower, a Dragon, a Lord, or the enemy base.

**Fix:** After winning a fight, immediately look for the closest objective. Towers > Lord > Dragon > enemy jungle camps. Push your advantage. Don't let them respawn and reset.

---

## Quick Takeaways

- Don't fight without ults. Check those green dots.
- When behind, look for picks, not 5v5s.
- Check your minimap before engaging. Your ADC might be farming.
- Know your role and hit the right targets.
- Save burst for carries, not tanks.
- Respect power spikes. Some heroes are just stronger at certain points.
- Win the fight → take an objective → win the game.
