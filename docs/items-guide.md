# Honor of Kings Items Guide: What to Build and Why

*by Marcus Tran · Updated June 2026 · 10 min read*

---

Items in Honor of Kings are how you turn your hero from "mildly annoying" to "delete button." But the shop has over 100 items, and if you don't know what you're looking for, it's overwhelming.

This guide explains the item system, what stats matter, and how to choose the right items for any situation.

---

## Item Categories

Items fall into five broad categories based on what stats they provide:

### Attack Items (Physical)
For ADCs, fighters, and assassins who deal physical damage.
- **Key stats:** Physical Attack, Attack Speed, Crit Rate, Armor Penetration
- **Examples:** Endless Blade, Spiked Shell, Axe of Torment, Shadow Ripper

### Magic Items
For mages who deal magic damage.
- **Key stats:** Ability Power, Magic Penetration, Cooldown Reduction
- **Examples:** Breakthrough Robe, Blood Clan's Grimoire, Augur's Word, Cloud Piercer

### Defense Items (Armor)
Against physical damage dealers.
- **Key stats:** Physical Defense, Health
- **Examples:** Cuirass of Savagery, Blazing Cape, Belt of Might

### Defense Items (Magic Resist)
Against magic damage dealers.
- **Key stats:** Magic Defense, Health
- **Examples:** Blood Rage, Anti-magic Cloak, Crimson Shadow

### Boots
Every hero buys boots. They're cheap and provide essential stats.
- **Examples:** Boots of Resistance (CC reduction), Boots of Dexterity (attack speed), Boots of the Arcane (magic pen), Hunter Boots (jungle)

## Core Items for Each Role

### ADCs (Marksmen)
1. Boots of Dexterity — attack speed
2. Spiked Shell — attack speed + passive damage
3. Endless Blade — crit damage
4. Blade of Eternity — armor pen
5. Cloak of Destiny — crit damage
6. Situational (Shadow Ripper, Bloodweeper, Dawnlight)

### Mages
1. Boots of the Arcane — magic pen
2. Breakthrough Robe — magic pen
3. Blood Clan's Grimoire — AP + CDR
4. Augur's Word — massive AP
5. Cloud Piercer — magic pen
6. Situational (Dawnlight, Crimson Shadow)

### Fighters (Clash Lane)
1. Boots of Resistance — CC reduction
2. Axe of Torment — armor pen + CDR
3. Cuirass of Savagery — armor
4. Blazing Cape — armor + burn
5. Blood Rage — magic resist
6. Situational (Endless Blade, Belt of Might)

### Assassins (Jungle)
1. Hunter Boots — jungle speed
2. Spiked Shell or Axe of Torment — damage
3. Endless Blade — crit
4. Blade of Eternity — armor pen
5. Shadow Ripper — armor pen + CDR
6. Situational (Cloak of Destiny, Bloodweeper)

### Tanks (Support/Clash)
1. Boots of Resistance
2. Cuirass of Savagery
3. Blazing Cape
4. Blood Rage
5. Belt of Might
6. Situational (Crimson Shadow, Dawnlight)

## How to Adapt Your Build

The recommended builds are a starting point. Good players adapt based on the game:

- **Enemy has lots of healing?** Build anti-heal (items with Grievous Wounds)
- **Enemy has lots of armor?** Build more armor penetration
- **Enemy has lots of magic damage?** Build magic resist earlier
- **You're getting bursted?** Build a defensive item earlier than usual
- **You're ahead?** Build more damage to snowball your lead

---

## Items to Know by Name

Some items are so important that you should know them by name:

- **Endless Blade:** The crit item. Almost every physical damage dealer builds this.
- **Axe of Torment:** Armor pen + cooldown reduction. Core on fighters.
- **Cuirass of Savagery:** The armor item. If you need physical defense, this is your first buy.
- **Blood Rage:** The magic resist item. Build this against fed mages.
- **Spiked Shell:** Attack speed + passive damage. Core on ADCs and some junglers.

---

## Quick Takeaways

- Use recommended builds while learning
- Adapt your build based on the enemy team
- Build defense if you're dying too fast
- Build penetration if the enemy is stacking armor or magic resist
- Know the key items by name — it'll help you communicate with your team
