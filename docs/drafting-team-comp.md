# How to Draft a Winning Team Comp in Honor of Kings

*by Marcus Tran · Updated June 2026 · 10 min read*

---

Most Honor of Kings games are lost in the draft before anyone even loads into the map. You've seen it: your team locks four squishy damage dealers and nobody picks a tank. Or you have three mages and no physical damage. Or — my personal favorite — someone picks a hero they've never played because "trust me bro."

Here's how to draft a team comp that actually wins games.

---

## The Basic Formula

A balanced team comp needs:

1. **One tank or tanky fighter** — someone who can frontline and absorb damage
2. **One marksman (ADC)** — sustained physical damage for objectives and late game
3. **One mage** — burst or control magic damage
4. **One jungler** — map pressure, objective control, ganks
5. **One support** — vision, peel, utility

This is the standard comp. Variations exist — double tank, double mage, assassin support — but if you're in solo queue, stick to the basics.

---

## Damage Split: Physical vs Magic

If your team is all physical damage, the enemy team builds one armor item and your entire comp is useless. If you're all magic damage, same thing with magic resist.

A good comp has at least one physical carry and one magic damage threat. Usually this is your ADC (physical) and your mid laner (magic), but it can vary — some junglers deal magic damage (Kongming), some Clash Laners deal magic damage (Mi Yue).

**Rule of thumb:** At least two sources of physical damage and two sources of magic damage. If you're the last pick and your team has no magic damage, pick a mage even if you're playing Clash Lane.

---

## Frontline vs Backline

Your team needs someone to stand in front. That someone is usually the Clash Laner (fighter/tank) or the Support (tank). If neither of them is tanky, your ADC and mage will get dove every fight with no protection.

**Minimum:** One frontline. Ideal: two (tank support + bruiser Clash Laner).

If your team locks four squishy heroes and you're last pick, lock a tank. I know you wanted to play Han Xin. Your team will thank you.

---

## CC (Crowd Control) Budget

CC wins games. The more CC your team has, the easier fights are. Stuns, knockups, silences, slows — all of it matters.

A team with zero CC (think: all assassins and marksmen) has to win through raw damage. One CC from the enemy team and your entire comp falls apart.

**Aim for:** At least three sources of hard CC (stuns, knockups, suppressions) across your team. Soft CC (slows) is a bonus.

---

## Engage vs Poke vs Protect

Team comps fall into three broad strategies:

### Engage Comp
You have strong initiation tools (Sun Ce, Lu Bu, Dun, Zhang Fei). Your goal is to start fights on your terms, dive their backline, and win through superior initiation.

**Needs:** Strong frontline, reliable engage, follow-up damage.

### Poke Comp
You have long-range damage (Marco Polo, Lady Sun, Milady, Nuwa). Your goal is to chip the enemy down before fights, then clean up when they're low.

**Needs:** Range advantage, disengage tools, vision control.

### Protect Comp
You have a hypercarry (Arli, Hou Yi, Consort Yu) and your entire team exists to keep them alive. Yaria, Zhang Fei, Cai Yan — everyone protects the ADC.

**Needs:** A hypercarry who can actually carry, multiple sources of peel.

Most solo queue teams are hybrid comps. But knowing your comp's identity helps you make better decisions. If you have a poke comp, don't force hard engages. If you have an engage comp, don't sit back and poke.

---

## Common Draft Mistakes

1. **No frontline.** Four squishies + one fighter who builds full damage. You lose every team fight.

2. **All AD or all AP.** Enemy builds one defensive item and your damage disappears.

3. **No CC.** You can't stop the enemy Fatih from running through your entire team.

4. **Three mages.** Two is pushing it. Three is trolling. Your ADC will have no peel and the enemy will stack magic resist.

5. **First-timing in ranked.** Please. Don't.

---

## Quick Takeaways

- Balanced comp: tank, ADC, mage, jungler, support
- Split your damage between physical and magic
- At least one frontline, ideally two
- At least three sources of hard CC
- Know if your comp wants to engage, poke, or protect
- Don't first-time heroes in ranked
