# Honor of Kings Beginner Guide: Everything You Need to Know in 2026

*by Marcus Tran · Updated June 2026 · 12 min read*

---

Starting Honor of Kings in 2026? Good timing. The game has been out globally for a while now, which means the meta is stable, the guides are written, and the community knows what works. But it also means you're jumping into a game where some players have years of experience.

This guide covers everything you need to know to get from your first game to your first ranked win — without wanting to uninstall.

---

## The Basics

Honor of Kings is a 5v5 MOBA (Multiplayer Online Battle Arena). Two teams of five players each pick heroes and fight to destroy the enemy's crystal (base). The map has three lanes — Clash (top), Mid, and Farm (bottom) — connected by a jungle filled with neutral monsters.

Games last 15-25 minutes on average. The team that destroys the enemy crystal wins.

---

## The Five Roles

### Clash Lane (Top)

You're on an island. It's you vs. the enemy Clash Laner for most of the early game. Your job is to win your lane, pressure towers, and join team fights in the mid to late game.

**Typical heroes:** Fighters and tanks (Lu Bu, Mulan, Dun, Sun Ce)
**Difficulty:** Medium. You need to know how to trade and when to rotate.

### Mid Lane

The shortest lane between the two bases. You're in the center of the action and expected to roam to other lanes. Your job is to control the map, deal magic damage, and support your jungler.

**Typical heroes:** Mages (Angela, Daji, Diaochan, Kongming)
**Difficulty:** Medium. You need map awareness and roaming timing.

### Farm Lane (Bottom)

You're the ADC (Attack Damage Carry). Your job is to farm gold, scale into the late game, and become the primary source of sustained damage. You're also the squishiest member of the team.

**Typical heroes:** Marksmen (Hou Yi, Marco Polo, Arli, Consort Yu)
**Difficulty:** Hard. Positioning is everything. One mistake and you're dead.

### Jungle

You don't have a lane. You farm neutral monsters in the jungle, gank lanes to help your teammates, and secure objectives like Dragon and Lord. You're the playmaker.

**Typical heroes:** Assassins and fighters (Fatih, Lam, Li Bai, Han Xin)
**Difficulty:** Very hard. You need to know every hero's strengths, jungle pathing, and gank timing.

### Roaming (Support)

You don't farm. You provide vision, peel for your carries, and enable your team. You're the unsung hero.

**Typical heroes:** Tanks and enchanters (Zhang Fei, Donghuang, Da Qiao, Yaria)
**Difficulty:** Easy to learn, hard to master. Your impact is invisible but massive.

## The Map

### Towers
Three towers per lane. They protect your base and deal increasing damage to enemies who dive. Don't tank tower shots early — they hurt.

### Jungle Camps
Neutral monsters that give gold and experience. Red buff (top side) gives a slow and burn on auto-attacks. Blue buff (bottom side) gives mana regen and cooldown reduction.

### Dragon
Spawns at 2 minutes in the river near Farm Lane. Killing it gives your team permanent stat bonuses. Contest it with your team.

### Lord
Spawns later in the game. Killing it summons a powerful minion that pushes a lane. Lord buff can end games.

## Gold and Items

You earn gold by:
- Killing minions (last-hitting gives bonus gold)
- Killing jungle monsters
- Killing or assisting in killing enemy heroes
- Destroying towers

Spend gold on items. Items give you stats (attack damage, ability power, health, armor, etc.) and unique effects. A hero with two items will beat a hero with one item almost every time.

**Beginner tip:** Use the recommended builds in the shop. They're not perfect, but they're good enough while you're learning.

---

## Arcana

Arcana are like runes — passive stat bonuses you equip before the game. You unlock arcana slots as you level up your account.

**Beginner tip:** Focus on one set of arcana first. Physical damage heroes all use similar arcana (attack speed, armor pen, crit). Magic damage heroes use a different set. Build your physical arcana page first — it works for ADCs, fighters, and assassins.

## 10 Tips for New Players

1. **Start with easy heroes.** Hou Yi (ADC), Angela (Mid), Cai Yan (Support), Arthur (Clash). Learn the game before you learn hard heroes.

2. **Last-hit minions.** You get more gold if you land the killing blow. It matters more than you think.

3. **Look at the minimap.** Every 5 seconds, glance at the minimap. Where is the enemy jungler? Where are your teammates? Map awareness wins games.

4. **Don't chase kills.** If the enemy is running to their tower with 10% HP, let them go. Chasing means you might run into their entire team.

5. **Ward bushes.** The little eye icons on the map let you place wards that reveal enemies. Use them. They're free.

6. **Group up after 10 minutes.** The laning phase ends around the 10-minute mark. After that, stick with your team.

7. **Don't fight when you're behind.** If the enemy team has more gold and items than you, play defensively. Defend towers. Farm. Wait for a mistake.

8. **Objectives win games, not kills.** A 20-kill lead means nothing if you lose every Dragon and Lord. Play for objectives.

9. **Mute toxic players.** If someone is flaming you in chat, mute them. They're not helping.

10. **Play to improve, not to win.** You're going to lose games. That's fine. Focus on learning one thing from each loss and you'll improve faster than players who just blame their team.

## Your First 10 Games

Here's a roadmap for your first 10 games:

1. **Games 1-3:** Play Hou Yi in Farm Lane. Focus on last-hitting minions and not dying.
2. **Games 4-6:** Try Angela in Mid Lane. Learn to roam and use your abilities.
3. **Games 7-8:** Play Cai Yan or Arthur. Learn what support and Clash Lane feel like.
4. **Games 9-10:** Try one game of jungle (start with Butterfly — she's the easiest jungler). You'll probably feed. That's okay. Jungling takes practice.

After 10 games, you'll have a feel for each role. Pick the one you enjoyed most and focus on it.

---

## Quick Takeaways

- Learn one role at a time. Don't try to master everything.
- Use recommended builds and arcana while learning.
- Last-hit minions. Gold matters.
- Look at the minimap. A lot.
- Objectives > kills.
- Mute toxic players. Enjoy the game.
