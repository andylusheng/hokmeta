# Best Junglers in Honor of Kings 2026: S-Tier Picks for Every Playstyle

*by Marcus Tran · Updated June 2026 · 8 min read*

---

The jungle role is the most impactful position in Honor of Kings right now. A good jungler controls the map, snowballs lanes, secures objectives, and makes the enemy team play scared. A bad jungler gets invaded, loses every objective, and gets flamed by all four teammates simultaneously.

Here are the best junglers in June 2026, broken down by playstyle.

---

## S-Tier: The Jungle Kings

### Fatih — The Best Jungler in the Game

Fatih is S-tier for a reason. His clear speed is the fastest in the game, his damage is absurd, and his mobility lets him invade, gank, and escape with zero risk. If Fatih is open and you're not picking him, you're trolling.

**Playstyle:** Aggressive invader. Start Red, invade enemy Blue at level 2. Kill the enemy jungler or steal their camps. Repeat until they're 3 levels behind and you've won.

**Weaknesses:** Point-and-click CC (Donghuang, Daji). If he gets locked down, he dies. But good Fatih players play around CC cooldowns.

### Lam — The Snowball Machine

Lam is the definition of a snowball jungler. Get one kill early and the game is over. His mobility and burst damage let him chain kills across the map faster than any other jungler.

**Playstyle:** Gank-heavy. Farm to level 4, then gank every lane on cooldown. Lam doesn't want to farm — he wants kills.

**Weaknesses:** Falls off if he doesn't get early kills. CC-heavy comps shut him down.

### Musashi — The Duelist

Musashi is the best 1v1 jungler in the game. He wins every duel, outpaces every clear, and can solo-carry games through pure mechanical outplay.

**Playstyle:** Invade and duel. Find the enemy jungler, kill them, take their camps. Repeat.

**Weaknesses:** Teamfight-reliant comps can outscale him if he doesn't close out the game early.

### Sun Ce — The Flex Pick

Sun Ce is unique because he works in both Jungle and Clash Lane. His engage is one of the best in the game, and his ultimate lets him join fights from across the map.

**Playstyle:** Objective-focused. Secure every Dragon and Lord with your teamfight ultimate. Gank lanes with your boat.

**Weaknesses:** Predictable engage pattern. Good players can dodge his ultimate.

---

## A-Tier: Strong Alternatives

### Prince of Lanling — The Invisible Assassin

Lanling is the best pick into squishy enemy comps. Go invisible, delete the carry, win the fight. Simple.

**Best against:** Angela, Daji, Hou Yi, Luban No.7 — any immobile squishy.

### Arke — The Reset Machine

Arke's passive resets her skills on kill or assist, which means she can chain kills in teamfights better than any other assassin. One kill leads to a pentakill.

**Best against:** Disorganized teams that don't peel for their carries.

### Nezha — The Global Threat

Nezha's ultimate is a global-range dive. He can be farming top lane and diving bot lane three seconds later. Great for punishing overextended carries.

**Best against:** Split-push comps, isolated carries.

---

## B-Tier: Viable But Outclassed

Han Xin, Li Bai, Wukong, Butterfly, Luna, Zilong, Kaizer — these junglers can carry games, but they're outclassed by the S and A tier picks. Pick them if you're a one-trick or if the meta shifts in their favor.

---

## Which Jungler Should You Play?

- **Want to climb fast?** Learn Fatih. He's the best jungler and will be until he gets nerfed.
- **Like assassins?** Prince of Lanling or Arke.
- **Prefer fighters?** Musashi or Sun Ce.
- **Want a pocket pick?** Nezha for global pressure.
