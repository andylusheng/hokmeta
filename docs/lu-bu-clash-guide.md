# Lu Bu Guide: Still One of the Best Fighters After All These Patches?

*by Marcus Tran · Updated June 2026 · 11 min read*

---

Lu Bu has been around forever. He's been nerfed, buffed, reworked slightly, and nerfed again — and he's still showing up in Clash Lane at every rank. There's a reason for that.

He's not the flashiest fighter. He doesn't have Li Bai's dashes or Han Xin's mobility. What he has is a kit that just works — true damage, sustain, a massive teamfight ultimate, and enough tankiness to frontline when your team refuses to pick a real tank.

I've played Lu Bu for five seasons straight. Here's what you actually need to know.

---

## Why Lu Bu Still Works

Lu Bu's entire value proposition comes from two things:

1. **True damage.** After using a skill, his next basic attack deals true damage and heals him. True damage ignores armor. Tanks, bruisers, squishies — doesn't matter. You hit them, they take full damage.
2. **His ultimate.** Lu Bu's ult is a massive AoE leap that knocks enemies up on landing and creates a zone that buffs his resistances and heals him. A well-placed Lu Bu ult wins teamfights. A badly placed one loses them.

He's also surprisingly hard to kill. Between his Skill 2 shield, his passive healing, and his ult's defensive buffs, Lu Bu can survive situations that would delete other fighters.

The tradeoff? He's slow. No dashes (except his ult). If you get caught out of position, you're probably dead.

---

## Skills Breakdown

### Passive: Martial Spirit

After casting a skill, Lu Bu's next basic attack deals bonus true damage and heals him for a percentage of his lost HP. This heal scales with missing health, which means the lower you are, the more you heal.

This is the reason Lu Bu can win extended trades that he has no business winning. You get low, you heal back up, they stay low. The key is spacing your skills so you're always getting that enhanced auto between casts.

### Skill 1: Sky Sweeper

Lu Bu sweeps his halberd in a wide arc. If it hits an enemy hero, he gets an attack speed buff and his next Skill 1 becomes empowered — dealing more damage and applying a slow.

This is your main damage tool. The empowered version hits hard. The attack speed buff means faster passive procs. In lane, you want to hit this on the enemy hero whenever it's up. Against minions it's fine too, but you lose the buff.

### Skill 2: Crescent Sweep

Lu Bu swings his weapon forward, dealing damage and gaining a shield. The shield amount increases based on how many enemies are hit.

This is your sustain in lane. Walk up to the wave, hit as many minions and the enemy hero as possible, get a fat shield, then trade. If you're getting poked down, use Skill 2 on the wave to shield up and heal with your passive autos.

### Skill 3 (Ultimate): Dynasty's End

Lu Bu leaps to a target location, dealing damage and knocking up enemies in a large area. He gains bonus physical and magic defense while inside the zone, and his skills heal him for a percentage of damage dealt.

This ult is a game-changer when used correctly. The knockup is instant and massive. The defensive buffs make you nearly unkillable for the duration. And the healing means you can turn around fights that looked completely lost.

But it has a cast time. You can be CC'd out of the jump. Smart enemies will save their CC for your ult animation.

---

## Laning Phase

Lu Bu wins most Clash Lane matchups through attrition. You don't burst anyone down. You chip them, shield their poke, heal back up, and eventually they have to base while you're still full HP.

Against ranged Clash laners (rare, but they exist): play safe until level 4. Your ult is your gap closer. Once you have it, you can jump on them and they can't run.

Against other fighters: trade when your Skill 1 is empowered. Use Skill 2 on the wave to shield before trading. Don't all-in unless they're below 40% HP and your ult is up.

---

## Teamfighting with Lu Bu

Your job in teamfights is simple: **land your ult on the backline.**

Don't use it to initiate unless you see a perfect opportunity. Let your tank or support engage first, then jump on their carries when they're distracted. The knockup + your damage is usually enough to kill a marksman or mage before they can react.

If your team has no engage, you can be the engage — but ping your team first. Nothing worse than ulting into five people while your team is backing off.

After your ult, stay on their carries. Your true damage ignores whatever defense they built. Your healing keeps you alive. The longer the fight goes, the better Lu Bu gets.

## Recommended Build

### Standard Fighter Build

1. Boots of Resistance — CC reduction is crucial for Lu Bu
2. Axe of Torment — armor pen and cooldown reduction
3. Cuirass of Savagery — armor and health, great midgame item
4. Blazing Cape — more armor plus burn damage in fights
5. Blood Rage — magic resist and a shield when you get low
6. Belt of Might — more health, more damage

### Aggressive Build (when you're ahead)

1. Boots of Resistance
2. Axe of Torment
3. Spiked Shell — attack speed for more passive procs
4. Endless Blade — your true damage crits now
5. Blazing Cape
6. Blood Rage or Cloak of Destiny

---

## Arcana Setup

- Hunt (x10) — attack speed and movement speed
- Fate (x10) — attack speed and crit
- Eagle Eye (x10) — armor pen

Some players run tank arcana (more HP, more defense). I don't recommend it. Lu Bu's sustain comes from dealing damage and healing — not from stacking HP. The more damage you deal, the more you heal.

---

## Matchups

### Lu Bu beats:
- Most tanks (Xiang Yu, Lian Po) — true damage ignores their armor
- Sustain fighters (Kaizer, Arthur) — you out-sustain them
- Slow bruisers (Dian Wei, Biron) — you kite them with Skill 1 and win extended trades

### Lu Bu struggles against:
- Guan Yu — he's faster, kites you, and his CC interrupts your ult
- Mulan — she can burst you before your sustain kicks in
- Ranged Clash laners — you can't hit them to heal

---

## Quick Takeaways

- Always auto-attack between skills to maximize passive healing and true damage
- Use Skill 2 on the wave to shield before trading
- Save your ult for the backline — don't waste it on tanks
- You scale. Don't tilt if you lose lane early. Lu Bu at three items is a different hero.
- CC is your biggest counter. Build Boots of Resistance every game.

Lu Bu isn't going anywhere. He's been meta-relevant since launch and will stay that way because his kit is fundamentally solid. Learn him, and you'll always have a reliable Clash Lane pick.
