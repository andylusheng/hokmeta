# The 5-Man Push Strategy That Works at Every Rank

*by Marcus Tran · Updated June 2026 · 8 min read*

---

There's a strategy in Honor of Kings that's so simple it feels like cheating. It works in Bronze, it works in Mythic, and it works against players who should know better. It's called the 5-man push, and here's how to execute it.

---

## What Is the 5-Man Push?

The 5-man push is exactly what it sounds like: your entire team groups up and pushes one lane together. No split pushing. No jungle farming. No "let me just grab this camp real quick." Five people, one lane, one objective: take the tower.

The enemy team has two choices:
1. Defend as five — and give up the other two lanes
2. Split up to defend multiple lanes — and get outnumbered 5v3 or 5v2

Either way, you win.

---

## When to Use It

The 5-man push is most effective when:

1. **Your team has a gold lead.** If you're ahead, you win the 5v5. Group up and force the enemy to fight you or give up towers.

2. **You just won a team fight and took Baron.** Baron-buffed minions are nearly impossible to defend against. Group up and end.

3. **The enemy team has a weak wave clear.** If the enemy can't clear waves quickly (no mage with AoE, ADC is behind), they can't defend against five people pushing one lane.

4. **You have a strong siege comp.** Heroes with long range (Marco Polo, Lady Sun, Milady) or tower-damaging abilities (Liu Shan, Luban No.7) make the 5-man push unstoppable.

---

## When NOT to Use It

1. **You're behind on gold.** Grouping up when you're behind just means you lose the 5v5 faster. Farm side lanes and look for picks instead.

2. **The enemy has a strong team fight comp.** If the enemy has Lu Bu, Da Qiao, and Fatih, they want you to group up. Don't give them what they want.

3. **Your side lanes are pushing into your base.** If you're grouping mid while super minions are hitting your inhibitor, you're about to lose the game.

---

## How to Execute It

### Step 1: Set Up the Map

Before you group, push out the side lanes. You want minion waves pushing toward the enemy base in at least two lanes. This forces the enemy to either split up to defend or lose towers for free.

### Step 2: Group and Push

All five players group in one lane — usually mid, but any lane works. Push the wave to the enemy tower. Your frontline stands in front of the tower, your damage dealers hit the tower from range, and your support provides vision behind you so you don't get flanked.

### Step 3: Siege the Tower

Don't dive. Just hit the tower. If the enemy tries to defend, your frontline zones them away while your ADC and mage chip the tower. If they engage, fight them — you have numbers advantage in that lane because they had to leave someone to defend side lanes.

### Step 4: Rotate

Once the tower falls, rotate to the next lane. Don't overstay. Don't chase kills. Take the tower and leave. Repeat until you're at their base.

---

## Why It Works

The 5-man push exploits a fundamental weakness of solo queue: lack of coordination. Most teams don't know how to respond to coordinated pressure. One person rotates to defend, another stays to farm, a third is typing in chat — and by the time they figure out what's happening, you've already taken two towers.

Even in coordinated teams, the 5-man push forces hard decisions. Do they give up towers or give up map control? Do they fight 5v5 or try to split push? Every choice has a downside.

---

## Quick Takeaways

- Group as five, push one lane, take the tower, rotate
- Use when ahead or after Baron
- Don't use when behind or against strong team fight comps
- Set up side lanes before grouping
- Don't dive towers — siege them
- Take the objective and leave. Don't chase kills.
