# Honor of Kings Jungle Guide for Beginners: From Picking to Carrying

*by Marcus Tran · Updated June 2026 · 11 min read*

---

Jungle is the hardest role in Honor of Kings. You have no lane, no tower to hide under, and four teammates who will blame you for everything. But it's also the most impactful role — a good jungler controls the map, snowballs lanes, and wins games.

This guide covers everything a new jungler needs to know: what heroes to pick, how to clear camps, when to gank, and how to not get flamed.

---

## What Does a Jungler Do?

Your job has four parts:

1. **Farm jungle camps** — You get gold and experience from neutral monsters, not lane minions.
2. **Gank lanes** — You show up in lanes to help your teammates kill the enemy or force them to recall.
3. **Secure objectives** — Dragons and Lords spawn in the river. You're responsible for taking them with your team.
4. **Control the map** — You track the enemy jungler, counter their ganks, and invade their jungle when you can.

---

## Best Beginner Junglers

Don't start with Li Bai or Han Xin. You'll feed, your team will flame you, and you'll never want to jungle again. Start with these:

### Butterfly — The Training Wheels Jungler

Butterfly is the easiest jungler in the game. Her clear is simple, her damage is consistent, and her passive resets her abilities on kills and assists. She's the perfect hero to learn jungle fundamentals on.

**Playstyle:** Farm to level 4, gank the most pushed-up lane, use your ult to finish kills, and watch your abilities reset for cleanup.

### Zilong — The Fighter Jungler

Zilong works in both Clash Lane and Jungle. He's tankier than most assassins, which means you can survive mistakes that would kill a Li Bai or Han Xin. His clear is decent and his ganks are straightforward.

**Playstyle:** Farm, dash in with Skill 1, knock them up, and beat them down with your ult active.

### Kaizer — The Bruiser Jungler

Kaizer is forgiving. He's tanky, he deals damage, and his ultimate makes him nearly unkillable for a few seconds. If you're learning jungle, Kaizer lets you make mistakes without instantly dying.

**Playstyle:** Farm to level 4, ult into a lane, and run at the enemy. Not complicated. Effective.

## Jungle Clear Order

Every jungler starts by clearing camps to hit level 4. The standard clear:

**Red Buff → Raptors → Wolves → Blue Buff → Toad → Level 4 → Gank**

### Why start Red Buff?
Red Buff gives your auto-attacks a slow and burn effect. This makes your ganks much more threatening. Starting Red also means you end your clear near Blue Buff, which puts you close to the Clash Lane or Mid Lane for your first gank.

### Why not start Blue Buff?
Some mana-hungry junglers start Blue. But for beginners, Red start is safer and more effective. You can always adapt once you understand matchups.

### Clear Speed Matters
Different junglers clear at different speeds. Fatih and Lam can full clear in about 1:20. Butterfly takes about 1:35. Zilong takes about 1:40. If the enemy jungler is faster, they'll hit level 4 before you and gank first. Be ready for that.

## When to Gank

Don't force ganks. A failed gank wastes time, reveals your position, and lets the enemy jungler take your camps or counter-gank another lane.

### Good Gank Opportunities:
1. **The enemy is pushed past river.** If they're near your tower, they can't escape easily.
2. **Your laner has CC.** If your Clash Laner is Mulan or your support is Donghuang, their CC makes your gank almost guaranteed.
3. **The enemy is low on HP or mana.** Low resources mean they can't fight back.
4. **The enemy jungler just showed on the other side of the map.** If you see them top, gank bot. They can't counter-gank.

### Bad Gank Opportunities:
1. **The enemy is under their tower.** You'll take tower shots and probably die.
2. **Your laner is low on HP.** They can't follow up.
3. **You don't know where the enemy jungler is.** They might be waiting to counter-gank.
4. **Your ultimate is on cooldown.** Wait for it.

## Objective Control

### Dragon (Spawns at 2:00)
Dragon gives permanent stat bonuses to your entire team. It's the most important early-game objective. You should be at Dragon within 10 seconds of it spawning, with your team.

**How to secure Dragon:**
1. Gank Farm Lane (bottom) around 1:30 to force the enemy ADC to recall or die.
2. Ping your team to group at Dragon at 1:50.
3. If the enemy contests, fight them. If they don't, take it for free.
4. Smite the Dragon to secure it — don't let the enemy jungler steal.

### Lord (Spawns later)
Lord gives your team a powerful minion that pushes a lane. Lord buff can end games. Only contest Lord when you have vision of the enemy team and a numbers advantage.

## How to Not Get Flamed

Jungle is the most blamed role. Here's how to minimize the hate:

1. **Ping before you gank.** Your laner needs to know you're coming. A simple "on my way" ping prevents "where was my jungler???" in chat.

2. **Don't tax the wave.** After a successful gank, help push the wave to the enemy tower, but don't last-hit the minions. Your laner needs that gold.

3. **Show up to objectives.** If Dragon is spawning and you're on the other side of the map farming wolves, you're doing it wrong.

4. **Mute toxic players.** If your 0/3 ADC is flaming you for not ganking enough, mute them. They're not helping.

5. **Accept that you'll get blamed.** Even if you play perfectly, someone will blame you. That's the jungle life.

---

## Quick Takeaways

- Start with easy junglers: Butterfly, Zilong, Kaizer
- Standard clear: Red Buff → Raptors → Wolves → Blue Buff → Toad → Gank
- Gank when the enemy is pushed up and your laner can follow
- Secure Dragon at 2:00 with your team
- Ping before ganking, don't tax waves, mute toxic players
