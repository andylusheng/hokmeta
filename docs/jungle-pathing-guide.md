# Jungle Pathing Explained: How to Clear Camps and Gank at the Same Time

*by Marcus Tran · Updated June 2026 · 10 min read*

---

Jungle pathing is the sequence of camps you clear and the routes you take between them. Good pathing means you hit level 4 faster, gank more effectively, and always have something to do. Bad pathing means you're behind on gold, late to every gank, and wondering why the enemy jungler is always one step ahead.

Here's how to path like a Mythic jungler.

---

## The Three Standard Paths

### Path 1: Full Clear (Farm to 4)

**Route:** Red Buff → Raptors → Wolves → Blue Buff → Toad → Gank

**When to use:** When you're playing a scaling jungler who needs level 4 to be effective. Most assassins (Li Bai, Han Xin, Wukong) and fighters (Zilong, Kaizer) want to full clear and gank at level 4.

**Pros:** Safe, consistent, guarantees level 4.
**Cons:** Predictable. Good enemies know exactly when you'll gank.

### Path 2: Red → Gank (Level 2 Cheese)

**Route:** Red Buff → Gank Mid or Clash Lane

**When to use:** When you're playing an early-game jungler (Fatih, Musashi) and the enemy mid laner is pushed up with no escape. A level 2 gank with Red Buff is surprisingly deadly.

**Pros:** Catches enemies off guard. Can get first blood.
**Cons:** If the gank fails, you're behind on farm and the enemy jungler knows your position.

### Path 3: Red → Invade (Aggressive)

**Route:** Red Buff → Invade enemy Blue Buff

**When to use:** When you're playing a strong early jungler (Fatih, Musashi, Sun Ce) against a weak early jungler (Butterfly, Dian Wei, Li Bai). You clear your Red, walk into their Blue side, and either kill them or steal their Blue.

**Pros:** Sets the enemy jungler behind. Establishes dominance.
**Cons:** Risky. If their laners rotate, you die.

## Reading the Enemy Jungler

Good junglers don't just clear their own camps — they track the enemy jungler and predict their movements.

### How to Track the Enemy Jungler:

1. **Check which buff they started.** If the enemy Clash Laner shows up to lane late, they leashed Blue Buff. If the Farm Laner shows up late, they leashed Red.

2. **Count their camps.** If you know they started Red, they're probably doing the standard full clear toward Blue. That means they'll be ganking the opposite side of the map at around 1:40.

3. **Ward their jungle.** Drop a ward at their Blue or Red buff at the start of the game. Now you know exactly where they started.

4. **Watch the minimap.** If the enemy jungler shows on one side of the map, they can't be on the other side. Use that information to gank or invade safely.

---

## Adapting Your Path

The standard paths are a starting point. Good junglers adapt based on what's happening:

- **Enemy laner is low HP?** Skip a camp and gank immediately.
- **Enemy jungler showed on the other side of the map?** Invade their jungle on the opposite side.
- **Your laner died and the enemy is pushing?** Rotate to catch the wave and defend the tower.
- **Dragon spawning in 30 seconds?** Reset, buy items, and path toward Dragon.

---

## Pathing for Specific Heroes

### Fast Clearers (Fatih, Lam, Musashi)
These junglers clear so fast that they can full clear and still gank before the enemy jungler finishes their clear. You can also invade at level 2-3 and kill weaker junglers.

**Recommended path:** Red → Invade enemy Blue → Gank → Clear your Blue side

### Slow Clearers (Butterfly, Dian Wei, Agudo)
These junglers need to play safe early. Don't invade. Don't force ganks. Full clear, hit level 4, and gank the most vulnerable lane.

**Recommended path:** Full clear → Gank → Recall → Repeat

### Gank-Heavy Junglers (Prince of Lanling, Nezha)
These junglers want to gank as early and often as possible. You can sacrifice some camps to get early kills, but don't fall too far behind on farm.

**Recommended path:** Red → Gank Mid → Clear Raptors → Gank Clash → Clear Blue side

---

## Common Pathing Mistakes

1. **Overstaying after a gank.** If the gank fails, leave. Don't stand in the lane waiting for something to happen.

2. **Ignoring camps on the way to a gank.** Clear camps as you path toward the lane you want to gank. Don't run past camps and leave them up.

3. **Ganking the wrong lane.** Gank the lane that's pushed up, not the lane that's pinging for help. Forced ganks rarely work.

4. **Not resetting.** After a gank or objective, recall, buy items, and heal. Don't wander around the map at half HP.

5. **Pathing predictably.** If the enemy knows you always gank Clash Lane at 1:40, they'll be waiting. Mix up your pathing.

---

## Quick Takeaways

- Full clear is the standard path for most junglers
- Red → Invade works against weak early junglers
- Track the enemy jungler by checking leash and warding their jungle
- Adapt your pathing based on lane states and objective timers
- Don't overstay after ganks. Farm, gank, reset, repeat.
